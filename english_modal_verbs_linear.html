The 4 Golden Rules

No Third Person -S

They stay the same for everyone.

She musts → She must

No "TO"

Followed by base verb (except ought to).

I can to swim → I can swim

No Auxiliaries

They make their own questions/negatives.

Do you can? → Can you?

No Tense Changes

No -ing, -ed, or infinitive forms.

I am musting → I must

Ability & Possibility

Present

Can

- I can speak French.
- He can run fast.
Past

Could

- I could swim when I was 4.
- They couldn't hear us.
Specific

Be able to

- I will be able to go.
- She has been able to win.
Pro Tip: Use "was able to" for specific past successes (e.g., "I was able to fix the car").

Advice & Suggestions

Should / Ought to

General recommendation.

- You should try this pizza.
- We ought to recycle more.

Had better

Strong warning + Consequence.

- You had better hurry or you'll miss it!
- We had better not be late.

Obligation

Must

Internal

Speaker's feeling or authority.

- I must stop eating chocolate.
- You must see that movie!

Have to

External

Rules, laws, circumstances.

- I have to wear a tie at work.
- Doctors have to work long hours.

The Danger Zone

Mustn't

Forbidden! Illegal or wrong.

- You mustn't smoke in here.
- We mustn't tell anyone.

Don't have to

Optional! Not necessary.

- It's free, you don't have to pay.
- You don't have to come if you're tired.

Logical Deduction

Must
95% Sure (+)

May/Might/Could
50% Possible

Can't
95% Sure (-)

"He has 3 Ferraris. He must be rich."
"The phone is ringing. It might be Tom."
"It's 40°C. It can't be snowing."

Past Modals

Modal + Have + Past Participle (V3)

Should have

Regret or criticism.

"I should have studied more!"

Must have

Deduction about past.

"He must have forgotten his phone."

Could have

Ability not used.

"I could have won, but I fell."

Needn't have

Wasted effort.

"You needn't have cleaned. I did it."

Requests & Permission

Informal: "Can I use your pen?"
Polite: "Could you help me, please?"
Formal: "May I request a meeting?"
Very Polite: "Would you mind opening the door?"

Past Habits

Used to

Habits & States.

- I used to live in Paris.
- I used to smoke.

Would

Only Repeated Actions.

- Every Sunday, we would go fishing.
- I would be a child ❌

Practice Time!

What is the missing modal?

"I'm not sure, but it _______ rain later."

Answer: Might / May

"You _______ touch that! It's electric!"

Answer: Mustn't
