Ability & Skills

CAN / CAN'T Present General Ability
  • I can play the violin.
  • She can't speak German.
COULD / COULDN'T Past General Ability
  • I could run faster when I was 10.
  • We couldn't find the exit.
BE ABLE TO Specific Success / Future / Perfect
  • I will be able to finish this.
  • They have been able to win.

Advice & Suggestions

SHOULD / OUGHT TO Recommendation / Opinion
  • You should try the local food.
  • We ought to recycle more.
HAD BETTER Strong Warning + Consequence
  • You had better study, or you will fail!
  • I had better go now or I'll be late.

Obligation

MUST Internal (I decide) / Very Strong
  • I must call my parents.
  • Visitors must sign in.
HAVE TO External (Rules/Laws)
  • I have to wear a uniform at school.
  • She has to work on Saturdays.
MUSTN'T Prohibition (Forbidden!)
  • You mustn't smoke in the hospital.

Logical Deduction Scale

CAN'T

99% Sure NO.
"It can't be him; he is in Tokyo."

MAY / MIGHT / COULD

50% Possibility.
"It might rain later today."

MUST

99% Sure YES.
"He has a Ferrari; he must be rich."

Past Modals (Modal + Have + V3)
SHOULD HAVE

Regret or criticism.
"I should have studied."

MUST HAVE

Past Logic.
"He must have forgotten."

COULD HAVE

Possible but not done.
"I could have won."

NEEDN'T HAVE

Unnecessary action.
"You needn't have cooked."
