MODAL VERBS

The Complete Visual Guide to Mastering English Auxiliaries

NO "-S" (He can)
NO "TO" (Must go)
NO AUXILIARIES (Can you?)
NO TENSE CHANGES

Ability

CAN
"I can speak English fluently."
COULD
"I could swim when I was 5 years old."
BE ABLE TO
"I will be able to help you tomorrow."

Advice

SHOULD
"You should sleep 8 hours a day."
OUGHT TO
"We ought to respect the environment."
HAD BETTER
"You'd better study or you'll fail!"

Obligation

MUST (Internal)
"I must stop smoking now."
HAVE TO (External)
"We have to wear a seatbelt."
MUSTN'T (Prohibition)
"You mustn't smoke in here."

Social

MAY / MIGHT (Permission)
"May I use your phone, please?"
WOULD (Polite Request)
"Would you mind helping me?"
SHALL (Offer/Suggest)
"Shall we go for a walk?"

Logical Deduction Scale

How sure are you about something?

CAN'T (0% No) MIGHT/MAY (50%) MUST (100% Yes)

"The phone is ringing. It might be John."

Past Modals (Modal + Have + V3)

Used for regrets, deductions, or criticisms about the past.

SHOULD HAVE
"I should have studied more." (Regret)
MUST HAVE
"He must have forgotten." (Logic)
COULD HAVE
"You could have won!" (Possibility)
NEEDN'T HAVE
"You needn't have cooked." (Wasted effort)

The Necessity Swap

DON'T HAVE TO
"It's free. You don't have to pay." (Optional)
USED TO vs WOULD
"I used to live here." (Habit/State)
"We would play every day." (Action)
