The "She's" Trap

She's happy
She IS happy
She's eaten
She HAS eaten

Rule: If the next word is a Verb (V3), "She's" means "She has".

Negative

Subject + HAVEN'T / HASN'T + VERB (V3)
  • I haven't seen that movie.
  • He hasn't called me.
  • They haven't arrived yet.

Interrogative

Flip the switch!

HAVE / HAS + Subject + VERB (V3) ?

Have you eaten?

Has she started?

Short Answers:

Yes, I have. No, I haven't.

The Battle

Past Simple vs. Present Perfect

Past Simple

FINISHED TIME

  • Yesterday
  • Last year
  • In 1999
  • When I was a child

"I lived in London in 2010."

(I don't live there now)

Present Perfect

UNFINISHED / RELEVANT

  • Since 2010
  • For 5 years
  • Lately / Recently
  • Just / Yet

"I have lived in London since 2010."

(I still live there!)

Signal Words

JUST
Very recent.
"I've just arrived."
ALREADY
Before expected.
"I've already finished."
YET
Not until now (Negative/Question).
"Have you finished yet?"
SINCE
Start point.
"Since 9:00 AM."
