Signal Word Radar

JUST: A moment ago.
"I've just eaten breakfast."
ALREADY: Sooner than expected.
"Wow, you've already finished!"
YET: Not until now (Neg/Quest).
"Has the postman arrived yet?"
STILL: Surprising continuation.
"I still haven't found it."

For vs Since

FOR

Duration (A period of time)

"I have lived here for 10 years."
  • For 2 hours
  • For a long time

SINCE

Starting Point (A specific date)

"I have lived here since 2013."
  • Since Monday
  • Since I was born

Been VS Gone

BEEN TO

Round trip.
You are back!

"He has been to Italy." (He is here now)

GONE TO

One way.
You are still there!

"He has gone to Italy." (He is in Italy)

The Battle

Past Simple VS Present Perfect

Past Simple

  • Time is DEAD.
  • "I lived in London in 2010."
    (I moved away. I don't live there now).

Present Perfect

  • Time is ALIVE.
  • "I have lived in London since 2010."
    (I still live there!).
